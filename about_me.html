Restore Image

Click here for notes
  • Pick an image, you can find some on google or wikipedia.
  • Save and download the image to photoshop
  • Find any spots you might think need restoring
  • Use the Quick Selection Tool: This tool (shortcut W) lets you "paint" over the subject to select it. It's great for subjects with a decent amount of contrast against the background.
  • Next use Spot Healing Brush Tool (J): This is your go-to for removing small blemishes, dust spots, and minor scratches.
  • Select and Mask: With your selection active, click the Select and Mask button in the options bar at the top. This will open a new workspace where you can use the Refine Edge
  • Then remove the original background
  • Create a Layer Mask: The safest and most common way to do this is with a Layer Mask. With your selection active, click the Add layer mask button (a rectangle with a circle inside) at the bottom of the Layers panel. This will hide the background, but not permanently delete it, allowing for easy edits later.
  • Add a new background
  • Place the Background: Drag your new background image into your Photoshop document.
  • Arrange Layers: In the Layers panel, drag the new background layer below the layer with your subject. This will place the subject on top of the new background.
  • Lastly, Resize and Adjust: Use the Move Tool (shortcut V) and Free Transform (Ctrl+T or Cmd+T) to resize, position, and rotate the new background until it looks right. You can also use adjustment layers to match the lighting and color of the new background to the subject.
