        <li>Use the Quick Selection Tool: This tool (shortcut W) lets you "paint" over the subject to select it. It's great for subjects with a decent amount of contrast against the background.</li>
        <li>Next use Spot Healing Brush Tool (J): This is your go-to for removing small blemishes, dust spots, and minor scratches.</li>

        <li>Then remove the original background</li>
        <li>Create a Layer Mask: The safest and most common way to do this is with a Layer Mask. With your selection active, click the Add layer mask button (a rectangle with a circle inside) at the bottom of the Layers panel. This will hide the background, but not permanently delete it, allowing for easy edits later.</li>
        <li>Add a new background</li>
        <li>Place the Background: Drag your new background image into your Photoshop document.</li>
        <li>Arrange Layers: In the Layers panel, drag the new background layer below the layer with your subject. This will place the subject on top of the new background.</li>
        <li>Lastly, Resize and Adjust: Use the Move Tool (shortcut V) and Free Transform (Ctrl+T or Cmd+T) to resize, position, and rotate the new background until it looks right. You can also use adjustment layers to match the lighting and color of the new background to the subject.</li>
